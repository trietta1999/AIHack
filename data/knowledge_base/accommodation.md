# Accommodation Overview & Pricing (2026)

Vietnam accommodation spans every price band. Indicative nightly rates for a double room in **off-peak season**:

| Tier | Hanoi / HCMC | Hoi An / Da Nang | Sapa / Ninh Binh | Phu Quoc |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hostel dorm | 150k–280k VND | 150k–250k VND | 120k–200k VND | 250k–400k VND |
| Budget hotel / 2★ | 400k–700k VND | 400k–650k VND | 350k–550k VND | 500k–900k VND |
| Boutique / 3★ | 800k–1.5M VND | 750k–1.4M VND | 700k–1.2M VND | 1.2M–2.5M VND |
| Luxury / 5★ | 3.5M–8M VND | 3.0M–7M VND | 2.5M–5M VND | 5M–18M VND |
| Resort villa | 6M–20M VND | 5M–18M VND | n/a | 8M–35M VND |

**Peak season** (mid-December to early January, Tet, 30/4–1/5, mid-June to August for domestic tourists): expect +30–80% mark-up; book 60+ days ahead.

**Cancellation tip**: most properties allow free cancellation up to 24–72h before check-in if booked direct on the hotel's website at the same rate as Booking.com or Agoda — and direct booking often includes a free upgrade.

# Homestays & Local Experiences

**Where homestays shine**:
- **Sapa region** (Ta Van, Lao Chai, Bac Ha): Hmong / Dao villages, USD 15–30 with dinner.
- **Mai Chau** (Hoa Binh): Thai stilt houses, 3h drive from Hanoi, USD 12–25.
- **Pu Luong nature reserve** (Thanh Hoa): rice paddies + mountains, USD 30–60.
- **Mekong Delta** (Ben Tre, Vinh Long, Can Tho): river island homestays, USD 20–40.

**Booking**: small homestays may not be on Booking.com — use VietnamStay, Local Vietnam, or contact via Facebook page. Always confirm:
- Toilet (squat vs Western, ensuite vs shared).
- Hot water availability.
- Wi-Fi (often absent in remote homestays).
- Whether meals are included.

**Etiquette**:
- Remove shoes before entering main house.
- Ask before photographing family altar / sleeping areas.
- Bring small gifts (school supplies, fruit) instead of cash tips.
- Drink the rice wine offered (or pretend convincingly).

# Hotel Booking Tips

**Platforms**:
- **Agoda** — strongest in Asia, frequent flash sales, integrates Vietnamese hotels well.
- **Booking.com** — broadest inventory, free-cancellation default.
- **Direct booking** — best for boutique / luxury; often includes upgrade or breakfast.
- **Klook** — bundles room + tour, e.g. Halong cruise + Hanoi hotel.

**Things to confirm before booking**:
- **Location**: Hanoi Old Quarter < 1 km radius is best for first-time visitors. HCMC District 1 (Ben Thanh / Dong Khoi area) for nightlife; District 3 quieter. Hoi An: inside Ancient Town (no cars) for atmosphere, An Bang Beach for relaxation.
- **Noise**: ground floors next to karaoke bars are common pain points; check recent reviews for "noisy".
- **Tax & breakfast**: rate may exclude 10% VAT + 5% service. Check whether breakfast is included.
- **Wi-Fi speed**: reviews mention this if it's slow.
- **AC quality**: a real concern in summer — confirm split AC, not portable.

**Avoid**: deeply discounted hotels with <10 reviews and no recent ones — common in newly opened low-quality buildings.
