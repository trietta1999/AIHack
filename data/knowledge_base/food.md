# Vietnamese Food — Must-Try Staples

**Pho** — rice noodle soup with beef (Pho bo) or chicken (Pho ga). Best regional differences: Hanoi pho is clear-broth with simple garnish (green onion, cilantro). Saigon pho is sweeter, served with a plate of bean sprouts, lime, sliced chilli, basil.

**Bun cha** — Hanoi specialty: grilled pork patties + pork belly in sweet-savoury fish-sauce broth, dipped with rice vermicelli and herbs. Bun Cha Huong Lien (Obama's stop) is famous; Bun Cha Dac Kim (Hang Manh St) is locals' favourite.

**Banh mi** — French baguette + Vietnamese fillings (pâté, cold cuts, pickled carrot/daikon, cilantro, chilli). Top picks: Banh Mi Phuong (Hoi An), Banh Mi 25 (Hanoi), Banh Mi Huynh Hoa (HCMC, line is long but real).

**Bun bo Hue** — spicy beef noodle soup, Central Vietnam. Annam Bun Bo Hue and Quan O Xuan (Hue) are classics.

**Cao lau** — Hoi An only; chewy noodles + pork + greens + crackers. Authenticity tied to local well water; outside Hoi An it's just imitation.

**Banh xeo** — crispy turmeric rice-flour pancake with shrimp + pork + bean sprouts. Eat wrapped in lettuce + herbs, dipped in fish sauce.

**Goi cuon** — fresh spring rolls with shrimp/pork, herbs, rice vermicelli in rice paper. Dip in peanut hoisin sauce.

**Com tam** — broken rice + grilled pork chop + egg + pickled vegetables. Southern Vietnam comfort food. Com Tam Ba Ghien (HCMC) is iconic.

**Egg coffee (ca phe trung)** — Hanoi invention, egg yolk whipped with condensed milk on top of strong Vietnamese coffee. Try at Cafe Giang.

# Street Food Safety

**Rules of thumb**:
- Choose stalls with high turnover and visible queue of locals — food doesn't sit out.
- Watch the cook prepare your dish; freshly cooked > pre-prepared.
- Avoid raw vegetables / ice if you have a sensitive stomach the first few days. Most reputable urban shops use bottled ice (look for cylindrical ice with a hole in the middle).
- Skip raw blood pudding (tiết canh) and undercooked pork — small but real food poisoning / parasite risk.
- Drink bottled or filtered water. Tap water in Vietnam is NOT safe to drink.

**Bring**: hand sanitiser, antidiarrheal (loperamide) for emergencies, rehydration sachets (oresol). Pharmacies are everywhere and cheap.

**Allergy phrases**:
- Tôi bị dị ứng với đậu phộng. = I'm allergic to peanuts.
- Tôi bị dị ứng với hải sản. = I'm allergic to seafood.
- Tôi ăn chay. = I'm vegetarian. (Note: even "vegetarian" dishes may contain fish sauce — say "ăn chay trường" for strict vegan.)
- Không cay. = Not spicy.

# Regional Cuisine Differences

**Northern (Hanoi, Halong, Sapa)** — restrained, less sweet, less spicy. Heavier use of black pepper, ginger, garlic. Soups dominate: pho, bun cha, bun thang. Influence from China.

**Central (Hue, Hoi An, Da Nang)** — most complex. Hue was the imperial capital — many tiny composed dishes (banh beo, banh bot loc, banh nam). Spicier than the north (chilli, lemongrass).

**Southern (HCMC, Mekong Delta)** — sweeter (palm sugar), more coconut milk, more fresh herbs, more tropical fruit. Banh xeo, hu tieu, ca kho to (caramelised fish in clay pot).

**Highland (Da Lat, Sapa, Buon Ma Thuot)** — barbecued meats, fresh vegetables, fermented foods. Specialty: thang co (Mong stew), com lam (rice in bamboo), Da Lat artichoke tea.
