# Vietnam Visa Overview (2026)

Vietnam offers three main entry channels for foreigners:

1. **Visa exemption** — 15-day to 90-day visa-free entry for citizens of selected countries.
2. **E-visa** — single or multiple-entry online visa, up to 90 days, available to all 80+ nationalities since August 2023.
3. **Visa-on-arrival (VOA)** — requires a pre-approval letter from a sponsor agency; mostly for air travellers landing at Hanoi, HCMC, Da Nang, Cam Ranh, Phu Quoc.

**Recommendation**: most leisure travellers should use the **e-visa** (cheapest, no agency middleman). VOA is mostly relevant for visa changes mid-trip or for travellers from agencies' partner countries.

# Visa Exemption List (as of 2026)

**45 days, single entry** (extended from 15 to 45 days in August 2023):
- Most Western European countries: UK, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Finland, Belarus, Russia.
- Japan, South Korea.

**21 days, single entry**:
- Philippines.

**14 days, single entry**:
- Brunei, Myanmar.

**30 days, single entry**:
- All ASEAN nationalities (Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, Laos, Cambodia).

**Phu Quoc-only 30-day exemption** — applies to **all** nationalities visiting Phu Quoc island only:
- Must enter and exit directly via Phu Quoc (international flight or boat).
- Cannot continue to other parts of Vietnam without obtaining a separate visa.
- Children listed on parent's passport included.

**For other nationalities** (USA, Australia, Canada, India, China, etc.): no visa exemption — use e-visa.

# E-Visa Application Walkthrough (2026)

**Official portal**: https://evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn (English available). **Beware** of third-party sites charging USD 50–100 mark-ups.

**Fees** (paid online by international card):
- Single entry: USD 25.
- Multiple entry: USD 50.

**Validity**: up to 90 days, single or multiple entry. Apply 7–14 days before travel; processing time officially 3 working days, in practice 3–5 working days.

**Required**:
- Passport with 6+ months validity remaining from intended date of exit.
- Passport bio page photo (PDF/JPG, <2 MB).
- Recent portrait photo (4×6 cm, white background, <2 MB).
- Intended port of entry and exit (can be changed at border on arrival).
- Address of first accommodation in Vietnam (any registered hotel address is fine).

**Tips**:
- Print 2 copies of the e-visa approval — one for airline check-in, one for immigration.
- Children need their own e-visa (not on parent's).
- E-visa cannot be extended in-country — exit and reapply, or convert to a longer visa via an agency.

# Visa Extension Inside Vietnam

**Visa-exempt entries** (45-day Western Europeans, 30-day ASEAN, etc.) **cannot be extended** in-country. Options:

1. **Border run** to Cambodia or Laos: cross at a land border, get a new 45-day stamp on return. Common at Moc Bai (HCMC → Phnom Penh, 8h) or Lao Bao (Hue → Savannakhet).
2. **Convert to e-visa**: apply for an e-visa while inside Vietnam, then leave and re-enter to activate. Adds 1 international hop.
3. **Convert to longer visa via agency**: 3-month tourist visa via local agency costs USD 80–150, processed by immigration office in 3–7 days.

**E-visa holders** can extend once for the same duration via licensed agency (USD 70–120 for 30-day extension).

**Overstay penalties**:
- 1–15 days: USD 25 fine at exit.
- 16–30 days: USD 25 fine + interrogation + warning stamp.
- 30+ days: deportation, 1–5 year re-entry ban.
