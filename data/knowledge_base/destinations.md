# Vietnam Top Destinations Overview

Vietnam has six widely-recommended destination clusters for first-time international visitors:

1. **Hanoi (North)** — capital city; Old Quarter, Hoan Kiem Lake, Train Street, Bat Trang ceramic village. Best months: October–April (cool, dry). Avoid July–August (hot, humid, occasional typhoons).
2. **Ha Long Bay & Lan Ha Bay (North)** — 1,600 limestone karst islets; UNESCO World Heritage. Day cruises from Halong city or 2D1N / 3D2N overnight cruises from Tuan Chau / Got pier. Lan Ha is the quieter southern extension accessible from Cat Ba island.
3. **Sapa & Northwest Highlands** — rice terraces, ethnic minority villages (Hmong, Dao). Best months: September–November (clear terraces) and March–May (green planting season). Reach via overnight sleeper train Hanoi → Lao Cai then 1h shuttle.
4. **Hue & Hoi An (Central)** — Hue: Nguyen dynasty imperial citadel + royal tombs. Hoi An: lantern-lit Ancient Town, tailor shops, An Bang beach. Hue ↔ Hoi An via Hai Van Pass (3h scenic drive) or rail.
5. **Da Nang (Central)** — beach city, Marble Mountains, Ba Na Hills (Golden Bridge). Modern infrastructure; convenient base between Hue and Hoi An.
6. **Ho Chi Minh City & Mekong Delta (South)** — HCMC for nightlife, history (War Remnants Museum, Cu Chi Tunnels). Mekong Delta day trips (Ben Tre, My Tho) or overnight stay (Can Tho floating markets).

Other notable destinations: **Phu Quoc** (beach island, 50-minute flight from HCMC, visa-free for 30 days), **Ninh Binh** (Trang An boat ride, often called "Ha Long on land"), **Dalat** (cool highland city, flower farms, coffee), **Phong Nha** (largest cave system in the world, Quang Binh province).

# Hanoi — Detailed Guide

**Recommended duration**: 2–3 days for first-time visitors.

**Must-see**:
- **Hoan Kiem Lake & Ngoc Son Temple** — sunrise walk, free entry to lakeside; temple ticket 30,000 VND.
- **Old Quarter (36 Streets)** — each street historically named after the trade (silk, silver, paper). Stay around Ma May / Hang Bac for walking access.
- **Temple of Literature** — Vietnam's first national university (1070). Ticket 30,000 VND. Allow 1–1.5 hours.
- **Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum complex** — open mornings only (Tue–Thu, Sat, Sun 8:00–11:00); closed Sept–Nov for maintenance. Dress code: covered shoulders + knees.
- **Train Street** (officially closed but cafés still operate) — train passes ~19:30 and 06:00. Sit only inside a registered café.
- **Water Puppet Theatre** — 1-hour show at Thang Long Theatre, tickets 100k–200k VND.

**Food**: Pho Bat Dan (Pho bo), Bun Cha Huong Lien (Obama's stop), Banh Mi 25, Egg Coffee at Giang Café, Bia Hoi corner at Ta Hien.

**Day trips**: Bat Trang ceramic village (45 min, 30k VND bus 47), Duong Lam ancient village (1.5h drive), Perfume Pagoda (2h drive + boat).

# Ha Long Bay — Cruise Guide

**Why cruise (vs day trip)**: overnight cruises reach quieter Bai Tu Long area, include kayaking, swimming, sunset on deck. Day trips stay in the crowded central bay.

**Cruise tiers (2026 prices, per person)**:
- Budget 2D1N (3-star): 2.0–3.5 mUSD VND equivalent ≈ 2,000,000–3,500,000 VND.
- Mid-range 2D1N (4-star, e.g. Paradise Elegance, Halong Sails): 4,500,000–6,500,000 VND.
- Luxury 2D1N (5-star, e.g. Heritage Bình Chuẩn, Stellar of the Seas): 8,000,000–14,000,000 VND.
- 3D2N adds Lan Ha / Cat Ba island stop; +50% over 2D1N price.

**Booking tips**:
- Avoid booking on the pier — quality varies widely; book through Klook, Viator, or Vietnam-based agents (Sinh Tourist, The Sinh Café).
- Confirm what's included: kayak rental, cooking class, transfer from Hanoi (4h coach), entrance fee (290,000 VND/person).
- Off-season (April–June, Sept–Oct) often gives 20–30% discount.

**Weather windows**:
- Best: October–April (cool, dry, calm sea).
- Avoid: July–August (typhoon risk; cruises cancelled with full refund usually).

**Reach from Hanoi**: 2.5–3.5h by coach (Limousine van service ~350k VND one way), or new highway 4h via local bus (~150k VND).

# Hoi An — Ancient Town Guide

**Recommended duration**: 2 full days + 1 night to see the lantern festival (15th of each lunar month).

**Highlights**:
- **Ancient Town walking tour** — Japanese Covered Bridge, Tan Ky old house, Fukian Assembly Hall. Combination ticket 120,000 VND covers 5 of 22 attractions, valid 24h.
- **An Bang Beach** — 4 km from town centre, calmer than Cua Dai (which is suffering erosion). Free beach chairs if you order a drink.
- **Tra Que Vegetable Village** — 3 km from town; cooking classes 750k–1.5M VND/person including market tour.
- **My Son Sanctuary** — Cham ruins, 1 hour drive; tour ~600k VND including transport + guide.
- **Lantern release on Thu Bon River** — 20,000 VND for a small lantern; ethically sourced biodegradable lanterns increasingly available.

**Tailor shops**: Yaly Couture, BeBe Tailor, A Dong Silk are well-reviewed. Allow 24–48h for first fitting; plan 3 days minimum if you want a suit or dress.

**Transport**: bicycles are free at most hotels; motorbike rental 150k VND/day. Taxi to Da Nang airport ~400k VND (45 min).

# Phu Quoc — Beach Island Guide

**Why go**: Vietnam's largest island, ~50-minute flight from HCMC or Hanoi, **30-day visa-free for most nationalities** (separate Phu Quoc-specific exemption — see visa.md).

**Best beaches**:
- **Sao Beach** (south) — fine white sand, calm water, busy weekends.
- **Long Beach** (west) — 20 km strip, sunset views, most resorts located here.
- **Ong Lang** (mid-west) — quieter alternative to Long Beach, mid-range resorts.
- **Starfish Beach (Bai Sao Bien)** (northwest) — shallow water, named for visible starfish (touch them gently, do NOT lift out of water).

**Activities**:
- Hon Thom cable car (world's longest over sea, 7.9 km) — 600,000 VND adult, includes Aquatopia waterpark.
- Snorkelling 3-island tour from An Thoi pier — 500k–800k VND.
- VinWonders + Safari combo — 1.6M VND adult, full day.
- Night market in Duong Dong — seafood, pearl jewellery.

**Best months**: November–April (dry). May–October is monsoon — rough seas, many tours cancelled.

**Avoid**: signing up for "free" sightseeing tours that end at a sales pitch for pearl shops or sim resorts.

# Sapa — Trekking & Ethnic Villages

**Reach from Hanoi**: overnight sleeper train (Hanoi → Lao Cai, 8h, ~500k VND for soft sleeper) then 1h shuttle to Sapa town (90k VND), OR direct sleeper bus (6h, 400k VND).

**Best trekking villages**:
- **Cat Cat** — closest (3 km), heavily touristic, paved path.
- **Lao Chai → Ta Van** — classic full-day trek (12 km, 6h), passes through Hmong and Giay villages.
- **Y Linh Ho → Lao Chai → Ta Van** — harder 1-day trek.
- **Ban Ho → Nam Toong → Thanh Phu** — 2-day homestay trek, less touristy.

**Best months**:
- **September–November**: golden rice terraces, clear weather.
- **March–May**: green planting season, cooler.
- **December–February**: cold (5–10°C), mist, occasional snow on Fansipan.

**Fansipan summit**:
- Cable car: 800,000 VND round trip + 70,000 VND funicular to base. Allow 4 hours.
- Trekking: 2D1N moderate difficulty; needs licensed guide (~3,500,000 VND including porter).

**Homestay etiquette**:
- Remove shoes before entering.
- Bring small gifts (notebooks, pens) for host's children rather than candy.
- Tip 50k–100k VND per guest per night beyond agreed rate.
