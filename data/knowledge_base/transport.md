# Domestic Flights in Vietnam

**Main carriers**: Vietnam Airlines (full-service), Vietjet Air (budget), Bamboo Airways (mid-tier). Pacific Airlines (Vietnam Airlines' budget subsidiary) operates a smaller schedule.

**Booking tips**:
- Book 30–60 days ahead for the cheapest fares; 7 days ahead can double the price.
- Vietjet sells "Eco" fares without baggage — add 20 kg checked bag online for ~250k VND, vs ~600k VND at the airport counter.
- Tet (Lunar New Year, late Jan – mid Feb) and 30/4–1/5 (Reunification Day) holidays sell out 60+ days ahead.

**Common routes & 2026 indicative one-way fares (economy, 30 days advance)**:
- Hanoi (HAN) ↔ HCMC (SGN): 1.5M–2.5M VND.
- Hanoi (HAN) ↔ Da Nang (DAD): 700k–1.5M VND.
- HCMC (SGN) ↔ Da Nang (DAD): 800k–1.6M VND.
- HCMC (SGN) ↔ Phu Quoc (PQC): 600k–1.3M VND.
- Hanoi (HAN) ↔ Phu Quoc (PQC): 1.5M–2.8M VND.

**Airport transfer**:
- Noi Bai (HAN): airport bus 86 (45k VND, 45 min to Old Quarter), Grab car (350k–450k VND).
- Tan Son Nhat (SGN): airport bus 49 (40k VND, 40 min to District 1), Grab car (200k–280k VND). Avoid pre-arranged taxi touts inside the arrivals hall.
- Da Nang (DAD): 10-minute Grab to city centre, ~80k VND.

# Vietnam Train Travel

The **Reunification Express (Hanoi → HCMC, 1,726 km)** runs four pairs of trains daily (SE1/SE2 … SE7/SE8). Total journey 30–34 hours; most travellers do segments rather than end-to-end.

**Popular segments (2026 indicative prices, soft sleeper 4-berth, one way)**:
- Hanoi → Lao Cai (for Sapa): 8h overnight, 500k–700k VND. Tourist-class carriages: Chapa Express, Victoria Express, Orient Express (+300k–500k vs state-run).
- Hanoi → Hue: 13h overnight, 700k–900k VND.
- Hanoi → Da Nang: 17h, 800k–1.0M VND.
- Hue → Da Nang: 3h, 100k–150k VND (sit in coach for the Hai Van Pass coastal views).
- Da Nang → Nha Trang: 9h, 400k–700k VND.
- Nha Trang → HCMC: 8h, 350k–600k VND.

**Booking**: dsvn.vn (Vietnam Railways official, accepts foreign cards), or Baolau / 12go.asia for English UI (~10% mark-up).

**Tip**: book 30+ days ahead for Sapa-bound overnight trains, especially weekends.

# Long-Distance Coach & Limousine

**Coach types**:
- **Sleeper coach (Xe giường nằm)** — 3 rows of bunks, used for overnight 6–12h routes. Popular operators: Phuong Trang (Futa Bus), Hoang Long, Sinh Tourist.
- **Limousine van (9-seat)** — premium 9-seat vans, doorstep pick-up, used for 2–4h routes (Hanoi ↔ Ninh Binh / Halong / Sapa). Price ~2x coach.
- **Open Tour Bus** — Hanoi ↔ Hue ↔ Hoi An ↔ Nha Trang ↔ HCMC tickets, hop-on hop-off style. Sinh Tourist most established.

**2026 indicative prices, one way**:
- Hanoi → Sapa (sleeper): 350k–450k VND, 6h.
- Hanoi → Ha Long (limousine): 280k–350k VND, 2.5h.
- Hanoi → Ninh Binh (limousine): 200k–280k VND, 2h.
- HCMC → Mui Ne (sleeper): 200k–300k VND, 5h.
- HCMC → Da Lat (sleeper or limousine): 280k–400k VND, 7h.
- Hue → Hoi An (open tour): 150k–200k VND, 3h via Hai Van Pass.

**Safety**: choose well-known operators only; sleeper coach accidents do happen on mountain roads at night.

# Getting Around in Cities

**Ride-hailing**: **Grab** is dominant nationwide; **Be** and **Xanh SM** (electric, owned by Vingroup) are credible alternatives. All accept cash or in-app card. Avoid hailing street taxis other than Mai Linh or Vinasun (HCMC) — meter-tampering common.

**Motorbike taxi (xe ôm)**: Grab Bike or Be Bike are cheap and bypass traffic; helmet provided. Sit upright, hold the side handle (not the driver).

**Renting a scooter**: ~120k–180k VND/day for 110cc automatic. Note:
- Foreign drivers technically need a Vietnamese-recognised licence (IDP 1968 Convention; Vietnam ratified in 2014). Police checks are common in Hoi An, Da Nang, Mui Ne — fine 800k–1.2M VND for unlicensed.
- Travel insurance often voids coverage for scooter accidents without a valid licence.

**Metro**:
- Hanoi: line 2A (Cat Linh ↔ Ha Dong) and line 3 (Nhon ↔ Hanoi Station) operating, 8k–15k VND.
- HCMC: line 1 (Ben Thanh ↔ Suoi Tien) opened December 2024, 7k–20k VND.

**Bicycle**: most hotels in Hoi An, Hue, Ninh Binh provide free bikes. HCMC and Hanoi NOT recommended for inexperienced cyclists due to traffic.
